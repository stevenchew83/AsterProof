Proof planning notes
  • Choose the main objects and relations before writing details.
  • Translate the desired conclusion into an equivalent condition.
  • Track reusable facts such as equal angles, cyclic points, and symmetry.
  • Connect each move back to the exact condition you need to prove.
  • Prefer claims that reduce the configuration instead of expanding it.
  • Record why each constructed point or line matters to the target.
  • Assuming a diagram relation before it has been justified.
  • Chasing too many facts without naming the next target.
  • Treating a special-looking diagram as a proof of the general case.
